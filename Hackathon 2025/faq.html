FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

What materials can I recycle?

Commonly recyclable materials include paper, cardboard, glass, plastic bottles, aluminum cans, and certain metals. Check your local recycling guidelines for specifics.

How should I prepare items for recycling?

Rinse containers to remove food residue, flatten cardboard boxes, and keep recyclables loose (not in plastic bags) unless otherwise specified by your recycling program.

Can I recycle plastic bags and wrap?

Plastic bags and wrap often can't go in curbside bins but can be recycled at designated drop-off locations found at many grocery stores.

What do the recycling symbols and numbers on plastic mean?

The symbols and numbers indicate the type of plastic and its recyclability. Some plastics are more widely accepted for recycling than others.

What should I do with electronic waste?

E-waste should be taken to special recycling facilities or collection events that handle electronics. Many electronics retailers also offer take-back programs.

Why is recycling important?

Recycling conserves natural resources, reduces pollution, saves energy, and reduces the amount of waste sent to landfills.

Can I recycle batteries?

Batteries should be recycled at designated collection points or hazardous waste facilities, as they can contain harmful chemicals.

How can I reduce waste besides recycling?

Reduce waste by reusing items, buying in bulk, choosing products with minimal packaging, and composting organic waste.

Is composting considered recycling?

Composting is a form of recycling for organic waste, turning food scraps and yard waste into nutrient-rich compost for gardening.

Where can I find more information about local recycling programs?

Visit your local government or waste management company's website for detailed information on recycling guidelines and programs in your area.
